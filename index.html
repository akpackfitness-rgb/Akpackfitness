FitnessKnowledge

Science-backed principles to train smarter, recover faster, and perform better.

Strength
Progressive Overload
The cornerstone of muscle growth. Gradually increase weight, reps, or sets over time. Your body only adapts when forced to handle greater stress than it's used to.
6–8weeks to see measurable strength gains
02
🔥
Metabolism
HIIT Burns Fat Fast
High-Intensity Interval Training creates an "afterburn" effect (EPOC). Your body continues burning calories for up to 24 hours post-workout at elevated rates.
24helevated metabolism after HIIT session
03
🥩
Nutrition
Protein Timing Matters
Consume 20–40g of protein within 45 minutes post-workout. This anabolic window maximizes muscle protein synthesis and accelerates recovery between sessions.
2.2gprotein per kg of bodyweight daily
04
😴
Recovery
Sleep Is Your Superpower
Growth hormone peaks during deep sleep. Muscles repair and grow while you rest. Less than 7 hours significantly reduces performance, testosterone, and recovery speed.
7–9hoptimal sleep for peak athletic recovery
05
💧
Hydration
Dehydration Kills Gains
A mere 2% drop in hydration reduces strength output by up to 19%. Water drives nutrient transport, joint lubrication, and core body temperature regulation.
3–4Ldaily water intake for active athletes
06
🧠
Mind-Muscle
The Mental Edge
Research shows focusing attention on the target muscle increases activation by up to 22%. Visualize the contraction before each rep — quality always beats quantity.
+22%muscle activation with mind-muscle focus
